Radiation Therapy Project   

  • Understand how radiation is used to treat cancer while protecting surrounding organs.
  • Develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills by analyzing real radiation therapy data.
  • Design a prototype to improve shielding or targeting for radiation therapy treatments.
  • Reflect on the ethical considerations involved in cancer treatment.

Guiding Activities

1. Case Study Analysis: Interpreting Dose Constraints

Understand how radiation is applied to treat a specific cancer area while minimizing exposure to nearby organs. Use the "boarding pass" documents provided to answer the following questions:

  • What is the primary treatment area specified in your boarding pass?
  • List the surrounding organs and their maximum dose limits.
  • Which organs need more protection and why?
  • Compare the maximum dose allowed for the cancerous area versus a nearby sensitive organ.

2. Problem-Solving Challenge: Designing an Improved Shielding Plan

Design a shielding plan that improves protection for a specific organ while maintaining effective treatment. Consider the dose constraints from your boarding pass as you answer the following:

  • Identify an organ that could benefit from additional shielding and explain why.
  • Describe your proposed shielding design and the materials you would use.
  • How would you test the effectiveness of your shielding prototype?
  • What challenges might arise when implementing your shielding design?

3. Comparative Analysis: Dose Limits Across Different Cancer Treatments

Compare dose constraints for similar organs across different treatment plans using multiple "boarding pass" documents:

  • Choose two treatment plans and list the dose limits for the same organ.
  • Create a table or chart summarizing the dose limits for each organ.
  • Explain why the dose limits vary between different treatment plans.

4. Hands-On Simulation: Creating a Radiation Map

Visualize how radiation is directed at the cancerous area and how nearby organs are protected:

  • Sketch a model of the primary treatment area and surrounding organs.
  • Use markers or string to show radiation beams.
  • Identify areas needing extra shielding and explain your choices.

5. Reflection and Ethical Discussion: Balancing Treatment and Side Effects

Reflect on the ethical considerations of radiation therapy:

  • What are the potential long-term side effects of radiation exposure to healthy tissues?
  • How would you balance the need to treat the tumor aggressively with minimizing side effects?
  • What ethical considerations must be kept in mind?

6. Integration with Prototype Development

Use real data to inform your prototype design decisions:

  • Which organ would you prioritize for protection in your prototype?
  • Describe the key features of your prototype.
  • How does your prototype balance treatment effectiveness with minimizing exposure?
