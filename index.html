Exploring Radiation Therapy Project

Welcome, innovators! This project will take you through an exciting journey of exploring the principles and applications of radiation in cancer treatment and shielding. Over four weeks, students will engage in research, collaborative problem-solving, and hands-on activities to understand how radiation is used in cancer therapy, its impact on human physiology, and how effective shielding can be designed.

Students will explore the use of radiation in treating different cancer types, including challenges related to minimizing exposure to healthy tissues and making treatment safer and more effective. Each group will have the opportunity to design a prototype that could enhance patient care, exploring solutions such as lightweight shielding or increased precision in radiation targeting.
